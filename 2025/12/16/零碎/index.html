Paper Summaries

2025arv-Mohammad-Atomic-scale probe of molecular magneto-electric coupling

Total: Motivated by the in-plane ferroelectric polarization of two-dimensional SnTe and the magnetic anisotropy of FePc molecules, the authors construct a FePc/SnTe heterostructure to investigate the magnetoelectric coupling at the atomic scale.
Method:

  1. scanning tunnelling microscopy (STM)
  2. inelastic tunnelling spectroscopy (IETS)

Process:

  1. SnTe flims are grown, followed by the deposition of FePt molecules on the surface.
  2. Ferroelectric domains and domain walls in bilayer SnTe are identified using high-resolution STM imaging.
  3. The authors obtain LUMO resonance (STM) and the first spin excitation energy (IETS), thus tracing the magnetic and electronic properties of FePc on different ferroelectric domains.
  4. A single FePc molecule is then manipulated across the ferroelectric domain wall using the STM tip, and measure LUMO and spin excitation energy through the process.
  5. DFT calculations are performed to support a high-spin ground state (S=2) of Fe, although dynamical magneto-electric effects are not explicitly captured.

The work mainly offers a static description and lacks dynamical information about how the ferroelectric switching influences the magnetic properties of the molecule.
